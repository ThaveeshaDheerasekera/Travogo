Nuwara Eliya

Nuwara Eliya is a city in the tea country hills of central Sri Lanka. The naturally landscaped Hakgala Botanical Gardens displays roses and tree ferns, and shelters monkeys and blue magpies. Nearby Seetha Amman Temple, a colorful Hindu shrine, is decorated with religious figures. Densely forested Galway's Land National Park is a sanctuary for endemic and migratory bird species, including bulbuls and flycatchers.

"The Moon Plains is a rather new area for hiking. As an open area full of fields, forests, walking paths and surrounding mountains it is a place loved by visitors who enjoy taking walks in nature or spending a day out in the open. Further down south after Ambewela and higher up the mountains is Horton Plains National Park, which includes the breath-taking and famous World’s End. The hiking trails in Horton Plains are top-notch and a must if you like hiking. World’s End is a drop-off at the edge of a huge cliff at the end of the Horton Plains plateau."

"Nuwara Eliya is a hill country, meaning that it is also a tea country. There are hundreds of acres of tea plantations in Nuwara Eliya and plenty of tea factories to visit. A little further away from town is St. Clair’s Falls, one of the widest falls in Sri Lanka and definitely worth a visit.Also consisting of the legendary Hakgala Botanical Gardens which are wildly believed to be the highest altitude botanical gardens in the world. On the road to Badulla,it would be a must to stop at the green gates and visit the beautifully manicured gardens full of roses and orchids and other plant species."

"Gregory Lake is a breathtaking lake in the middle of the Nuwara Eliya. With an calm park next to it with walkways and benches to rest on it would be an ideal place to visit. There are horse rides offered for an fair price of, a few hundred rupees, tourists can also 20-minute speedboat ride on the lake. The cool air is lovely on the water and the sights are unbeatable. Gregory Lake was created by Governor William Gregory between 1872 and 1877. Historically it is beleved to have used for watersports during the British colonial period."
